Postdoctoral Researcher

January 2021 - March 2022

Ghent University

My postdoctoral research built on my work as a PhD researcher. In particular, I investigated and developed methods for incorporating prior topological information into data embeddings, dimensionality reductions, and visualizations. Furthermore, I studied theoretical and practical complications when learning from noisy high-dimensional data that fall under the curse of dimensionality.

PhD Researcher

October 2016 - December 2020

Ghent University

My PhD research mainly involved developing new algorithms and methods for inferring, optimizing and visualizing topological models in 'graph-structured data'. In this sense, graph-structured data refers to either graphs themselves, or data that approximates such model, for example, the locations of earthquakes distributed across the globe. In particular, I focused on machine learning applications in bionformatics, namely inferring cell trajectory models from high-dimensional biological expression data of differentiating cells, which is of crucial importance to biomedical fields such as immunology and cancer research.

Visiting Student Researcher

March 2020 - May 2020

Stanford University

During my journey as a PhD researcher, I visited the Gevaert lab for multi-scale data fusion in oncology at Stanford University School of Medicine for three months. The purpose of this research visit was to actively collaborate and investigate new applications of topological machine learning methods to biomedical image segmentation and tumor histology prediction.
